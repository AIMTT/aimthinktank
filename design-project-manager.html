Design Project Manager

Full-Time

Greater Seattle Area

Position Summary

AIM THINK TANK, LLC seeks a Design Project Manager in Seattle, Washington to manage complex projects from site feasibility and entitlement strategy through construction documentation and permitting approvals.

Key Responsibilities

  • Manage complex projects from site feasibility and entitlement strategy through construction documentation and permitting approvals.
  • Lead the development of architectural and landscape design concepts, including feasibility studies, schematic design, and design development.
  • Produce and oversee high-quality construction documentation using AutoCAD and/or Revit, ensuring accuracy, constructability, and regulatory compliance.
  • Direct the preparation of 3D models, renderings, and visualization materials to support client decision-making and entitlement approvals.
  • Establish and maintain internal drawing standards, quality assurance and quality control processes, and documentation workflows.
  • Serve as the primary coordinator for civil, structural, MEP, arborist, and other specialty consultants.
  • Review and consolidate consultant deliverables into fully coordinated drawing sets.
  • Proactively identify and resolve scope gaps, conflicts, and coordination issues before submission.
  • Lead and manage permit and entitlement processes with the City of Seattle and other local jurisdictions.
  • Prepare and submit complete, code-compliant permit packages, including responses to correction notices.
  • Act as the primary point of contact with city reviewers, planners, and inspectors.
  • Provide strategic guidance on zoning constraints, land use regulations, and permitting risk mitigation.
  • Manage projects from site selection and due diligence through permit approval and construction support.
  • Develop and track project schedules, milestones, deliverables, and approval timelines.
  • Lead client meetings, design presentations, and progress updates with confidence and professionalism.
  • Advise clients on design options, entitlement risks, budget implications, and execution strategies.
  • Attend site visits, coordination meetings, and city reviews as required.
  • Support construction teams with clarifications, revisions, and field coordination as needed.

Minimum Requirements

  • Bachelor’s degree in Architecture, Landscape Architecture, Urban Planning, or a related field of study.
  • Twenty-four months of progressive post-baccalaureate experience with AutoCAD and Revit.
  • The position requires local travel to project sites and worksites within the Seattle–Bellevue–Tacoma metropolitan statistical area.

Employment Type

Full-Time
